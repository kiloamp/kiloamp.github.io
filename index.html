Stuff in my life right now


Formula Student

LUMotorsport is a big part of my university life. We have been working hard to prove that we can develop cool stuff at a reduced budget. Typically teams would pay around £1000 for a screen and up to £2000 for a telemetry system. By doing this in-house (of course it takes some more trial and error, but it is in the name of learning something new) we cut the screen cost to about £70, and the telemetry cost to a ridiculous £40. Awesome.

Football (as in soccer)

I think it's such a cool sport. You can play with anything from 2 to 22 (or well, unlimited really) people, you can run and use it as stamina training, you can sprint and 'fight' for the ball and use it as strength building, you can sit in the middle and try and spot possible passes and use it as a mental exercise, you can play in the cold UK weather and still be warm and it is generally a great way to meet people and to see self-progression as you are always pushed to do better.

Sim Racing

Arch Linux and ThinkPads

I think they are so neat. Such a kick in the face of today's standards of paying £1300 for a good computer, or £500 for a mediocre computer. Get an old second hand ThinkPad, put Arch on it and you are free! The cheapest ThinkPad I ever got was £4 (and a pack of chewing gum) and it still outperformed the most modern computers in certain senses. Be it for battery life, ergonomics, keyboard experience, raw speed (Arch to blame for this one), ser- (oh boy) serviceability (?) of the system. Great, just great.
